Pluralising Water Panel

Tavengwa Chitata

Panelist – Lecturer/Researcher, Water Governance, IHE Delft

Tavengwa Chitata is a lecturer and researcher in the Water Governance Department. His work examines how social, institutional and policy arrangements shape the use and management of water resources, particularly in agricultural and rural contexts in Southern Africa. He has contributed to regional projects on equitable water allocation, community participation and local knowledge in irrigation management, and is engaged in teaching on water governance and policy analysis.

Annelieke Duker

Panelist – Senior Lecturer/Researcher, Irrigation & Development, IHE Delft

Annelieke Duker works on farmer-led irrigation development and the social and institutional dimensions of agricultural water management. Her teaching and research focus on how smallholders innovate and adapt irrigation systems, and on creating enabling environments for inclusive water development. She collaborates closely with partners in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia to strengthen the links between science, policy and practice in irrigation and water governance.

a.duker@un-ihe.org Profile

Claire Michailovsky

Panelist – Senior Lecturer, Water Accounting & Productivity, IHE Delft

Claire Michailovsky is a senior lecturer in water accounting and productivity. Her research brings together hydrology, remote sensing and data analysis to better quantify water availability and use across river basins. She leads and contributes to international initiatives aimed at improving water productivity assessments and water accounting systems, supporting evidence-based planning for sustainable and equitable water management in agriculture and beyond.

Hadeel Hosney

Panelist – Lecturer/Researcher, Wastewater Treatment & Reuse, IHE Delft

Hadeel Hosney (Mahmoud) is a researcher and lecturer in wastewater engineering. Her work centres on the treatment and safe reuse of wastewater and on resource recovery technologies that contribute to circular economies. She has practical and academic experience in biological treatment processes, waste valorisation, and sustainable sanitation solutions, and she teaches and supervises MSc research in wastewater treatment and management.

Bota Sharipova

Panelist – PhD Candidate, Water Cooperation, IHE Delft

Bota Sharipova is a PhD researcher focusing on trust and cooperation in transboundary water management. Her work investigates the institutional and cultural factors that influence collaboration across river basins, drawing from political science and negotiation theory. Before joining IHE Delft, she worked in international water diplomacy and regional development, contributing to projects on shared water governance in Central Asia.

Assela Pathirana

Moderator – Associate Professor, Water Infrastructure Asset Management, IHE Delft

Assela Pathirana is an associate professor in the Department of Coastal and Urban Risk and Resilience. His work lies at the intersection of water infrastructure asset management, urban flood resilience and climate adaptation. He leads and contributes to international projects in Asia, the Pacific and the Caribbean, promoting practical, system-based approaches to sustainable water management. His recent work emphasises small island contexts as living laboratories for global resilience challenges.
